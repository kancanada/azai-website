What They Do

Salesforce, Inc. is a dominant player in cloud-based software, offering a suite of customer relationship management (CRM) tools and other enterprise applications that help businesses manage sales, service, marketing, and more. As a large-cap technology titan on the NYSE, its $173.37 billion market cap reflects its significant footprint in the application software sector. The company's strong earnings per share of $7.81, combined with a P/E of 23.7, suggest investors are willing to pay a premium for its established market position and growth potential.

Competitive Moat

Salesforce's competitive moat is primarily built on incredibly high switching costs and powerful network effects inherent in its integrated platform. Customers become deeply embedded in its ecosystem, making it both costly and complex to migrate to a competitor, which is a hallmark of a durable business. The strong Piotroski F-Score of 7/9 further reinforces the picture of a financially robust company with solid operational health, contributing to its enduring competitive advantage.
