Key Risks

The primary risk to consider is the inherent concentration within the S&P 500 itself, meaning a significant downturn in a few of its largest components, particularly in the technology sector, could disproportionately impact your returns. Additionally, as an equity ETF, VFV.TO is subject to market risk, meaning any broad economic downturn or recession will likely affect its performance. Given its U.S. market focus, currency fluctuations between the Canadian and U.S. dollar can also add another layer of risk.

📚

Overview

VFV.TO is a titan in the Canadian ETF landscape, boasting a substantial $28.29 billion in assets under management from Vanguard Investments Canada. This massive scale translates into excellent liquidity, making it easy to buy and sell shares without significantly impacting the price. With a current price of C$156.70 and a modest 0.92% yield, it offers a straightforward way to access the U.S. market.

🎯

Strategy

This ETF's strategy is elegantly simple: track the performance of the 500 largest U.S. public companies. By holding VFV.TO, you're essentially investing in a broad cross-section of the American economy, mirroring the S&P 500 index with impressive accuracy. The sector allocations, with a heavy emphasis on technology and financial services, reflect the current composition of the U.S. market itself.

💼

Top Holdings

It's worth noting that VFV.TO's top holding is the Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (VOO) itself, making up 100% of its assets. This structure means VFV.TO is essentially a Canadian-listed wrapper for the U.S.-based VOO, providing direct exposure to the S&P 500's constituent companies. Consequently, the fund's performance is intrinsically tied to the performance of these mega-cap U.S. stocks, with technology companies like Apple and Microsoft wielding significant influence.

Performance

VFV.TO has demonstrated solid long-term performance, with annualized returns of 18.9% over three years and 15.4% over five years, comfortably exceeding the 12% threshold for excellent performance. While it has experienced a modest 5.7% dip year-to-date, its 9.5% return over the past year highlights its ability to rebound and capture market upside. This consistent positive performance across various timeframes suggests a robust track record.

Costs

One of VFV.TO's most compelling features is its exceptionally low expense ratio of 0.08%, making it one of the most cost-effective ways to invest in the S&P 500. This minimal fee structure ensures that a vast majority of your investment returns stay in your pocket, which is a significant advantage over the long haul. While the yield is currently 0.92%, after accounting for the low fees, the net yield of approximately 0.84% is modest, so income generation isn't the primary draw here.
